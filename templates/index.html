About the Project

Intelligent Irrigation
for Modern Farming

A final-year engineering project merging embedded systems with AI analytics to solve water scarcity in paddy cultivation.

Paddy (rice) farming accounts for over 30 % of global freshwater usage. Traditional flood-irrigation wastes enormous amounts of water and increases greenhouse emissions. Our Smart Irrigation System replaces guesswork with precision sensor data and automated control.

The core hardware is an ESP32 microcontroller that continuously reads soil moisture, ambient temperature, and relative humidity from calibrated sensors. When readings cross defined thresholds, a 12 V water pump activates automatically — no manual intervention required.

All data is pushed over Wi-Fi to a cloud endpoint and visualised in real time on this web dashboard. A machine-learning module processes historical sensor logs and weather forecasts to predict irrigation schedules, saving water and boosting crop yield.
